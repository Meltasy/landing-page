The Pays Basque's hidden gem: Iroulegy wine
The Iroulegy mountain vineyard is one of the smallest in France, and the only one in French Pays Basque. Yet the wine produced here is exquisite - just one sip and you'll lose your heart!
Which grapes are used in Iroulegy wine?

Cabernet Franc is a black-skinned French wine grape variety. It's often found in Loire Valley and Bordeaux red wines.

Tannat is a black-skinned Basque wine grape variety. It comes from the region and is often found in South-West French red wines.

Courbu and Petit Courbu are a light-skinned Gascony wine grape variety. They come from the region and are often found in South-West French white wines.

Gros Manseng and Petit Manseng are a light-skinned South-West French wine grape variety. They come from the region and are often found in South-West French white wines.
